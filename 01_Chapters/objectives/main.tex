The main goal of this work is the design and development of a \gls{phantom} 
for the evaluation and calibration of \acrfull{eeg} signal acquisition systems.  
To achieve this goal, the following specific objectives are proposed:

\begin{enumerate}

\item Design a two-layered phantom head capable of accurately simulating the transmission of both brain-like signals
and standard test waveforms (e.g., sinusoids and square waves) through a physical medium. The internal layer will be
composed of a conductive gelatine, while the external layer will be made of conductive polymer. This phantom must incorporate
an internal signal generation system designed to maintain stable operation for a minimum test period of 
$X$~[days/hours].~\nota{Definir tiempo objetivo.}

\item Design, develop, and implement a desktop application platform for the operational integration and comprehensive management
of the proposed EEG signal phantom. This platform must provide a unified environment for:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Controlling signal generation parameters (e.g., amplitude, frequency, and waveform), \nota{Definir rangos objetivo}
    \item Real-time visualization of acquired and generated data with low latency and jitter~\nota{Definir rangos aceptables}, and
    \item Structured data logging and export (CSV, EDF, etc.).
\end{enumerate}

\item Design and implement the necessary communication standards and data structures for interoperability with the OpenBCI platform,
enabling the reliable reception and processing of real-time EEG data.

\end{enumerate}
