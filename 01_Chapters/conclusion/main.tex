This work presented the design and modular implementation and validation of a comprehensive EEG phantom head. While financial and manufacturing constraints caused a shift from a full physical integration to a component-based verification strategy, the results demonstrate that the proposed architecture satisfies the fundamental requirements for a cost-effective, reproducible, and adaptable research tool.

The investigation into material properties confirmed that saline gelatine serves as a viable material for the intracranial volume. The experimental characterization demonstrated that the material's electrical impedance is both tunable through salt concentration and reproducible across batches with a deviation below 10\%. Although temperature dependence introduces a variable that requires control during operation, the material provides a practical and accessible solution for academic prototyping and testing.

The numerical validation established the theoretical feasibility of the two-layer phantom design. The FEM analysis confirmed that internal signal injection results in measurable surface potentials, with significant attenuation, which characterizes the scale of the necessary amplification via the proposed electronic module. Furthermore, the stability analysis of the forward operator informed the critical design decision to reduce the actuation scheme from nine to eight electrodes, optimizing the system's numerical robustness without compromising spatial resolution.

The development of the software platform resulted in a desktop application capable of deterministic signal generation and real-time data acquisition. The adoption of a modern asynchronous architecture ensured good performance and seamless integration with OpenBCI hardware, successfully closing the loop between signal generation and acquisition.

The modular validation performed in this project lays the groundwork for the physical assembly of the complete phantom head. Future work should focus on the manufacturing of the conductive cranial shell and the integration of the identified forward model into the software. By embedding the numerical transfer operator directly into the application, the system will evolve from a signal generator into a calibrated simulation tool, capable of translating desired surface topologies into specific internal actuation patterns. This advancement will complete the transition of the platform from a validated prototype to a fully operational instrument for EEG research and calibration.
