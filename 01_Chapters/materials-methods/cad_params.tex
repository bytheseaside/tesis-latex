%-----------------------------------------------------------
% Parametric CAD Framework
%-----------------------------------------------------------

A fully parametric CAD workflow underpins the entire phantom-head model. 
Instead of fixed dimensions, all critical geometric features, tolerances, and mating conditions were defined through named parameters. 
This approach ensures global consistency, minimizes manual editing, and enables rapid regeneration of the model whenever design changes
 are required.

The parametrization affects all major components of the assembly. 
Examples include the shell thickness in the measuring region, the radius of the wire-guide channels, the width and depth of the sealing groove, and the height and keyed profile of the connector bases, along with their derived values.

The CAD model organizes parameters into two categories: system-wide parameters shared across all project files, and component-specific parameters defined locally within individual part files. Some parameters are user-defined input values, while others are automatically calculated from dependencies to maintain geometric consistency throughout the assembly.

Table~\ref{tab:system_params} lists the system-wide parameters that affect multiple components across the entire assembly.
Beyond these global parameters, component-specific parameters are defined within individual part files (Tables~\ref{tab:head_shell_params} 
and~\ref{tab:antenna_layout_params}). Although these parameters are localized to specific files—\texttt{printable\_hollow\_head} and
 \texttt{antenna\_layout}, respectively—they can influence not only the components they directly define but also any downstream parts
  that reference or depend on those geometries. This hierarchical organization ensures that changes to a base component propagate
   appropriately to dependent features while maintaining local control where needed.

\begin{table}[h!]
  \centering
  \caption{Head shell parametric variables (\texttt{printable\_hollow\_head}).}
  \label{tab:head_shell_params}
  \small
  \begin{tabular}{l@{\hspace{1em}}@{\extracolsep{\fill}}p{8cm}l}
    \hline
    \textbf{Parameter} & \textbf{Description} & \textbf{Value} \\
    \hline
    \texttt{opening\_height}  & Vertical distance from ground plane to the parting plane that divides the head shell into upper and lower halves & 72~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{groove\_width}  & Width of the channel for sealing material (gasket or liquid silicone) & 8~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{groove\_depth}  & Depth of the sealing channel in the flange. Calculated as $\mathrm{round}(0.7 \cdot \texttt{flange\_thickness})$ & 5~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{groove\_mid\_offset}  & Offset from flange internal border to the centerline of the sealing groove. Calculated as $(\texttt{groove\_width} / 2) + \texttt{shell\_thickness}$ & 7.2~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{mating\_mid\_offset}  & Offset from flange internal border to the midline of the male–female mating feature. Calculated as $(\texttt{flange\_offset} + \texttt{groove\_mid\_offset} + \texttt{groove\_width} / 2) / 2$ & 15.6~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{mating\_female\_width}  & Width of the female interlocking feature on the lower head shell half & 5~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{mating\_female\_depth}  & Depth of the female interlocking feature on the lower head shell half & 6~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{flange\_thickness}  & Vertical thickness of the flange tab extending from each head shell half & 7~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{flange\_offset}  & Horizontal distance from the head edge to the start of the flange tab & 20~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{mating\_male\_depth}  & Depth of the male interlocking feature on the upper head shell half. Calculated as $\texttt{mating\_female\_depth} - \texttt{mating\_tolerance\_mm}$ & 5.5~mm \\[2pt]
    \texttt{mating\_male\_width}  & Width of the male interlocking feature on the upper head shell half. Calculated as $\texttt{mating\_female\_width} - \texttt{mating\_tolerance\_mm}$ & 4.5~mm \\[2pt]
    \hline
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Any of these parameters may be modified to accommodate different fabrication processes, material choices, or experimental designs. 
Once updated, the model remains fully consistent and fabrication-ready without the need for manual geometric edits across related pieces.
