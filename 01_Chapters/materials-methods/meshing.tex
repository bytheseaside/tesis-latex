All phantom head components (shell, wire guides) were assembled computationally 
in CAD. For the electrodes, standard 3.5~mm Jack TRS connectors (model 
\textbf{SP-3533-02}~\cite{jack_datasheet}) were selected to provide realistic 
three-terminal (tip--ring--sleeve) voltage injection at each of the nine electrode 
sites, and 3D models of these connectors were incorporated to accurately represent 
the physical system.

With all components in place, the interior conductive medium volume was modeled 
by boolean-subtracting the wire guide and audio connector geometries from the 
total interior space, creating conformal electrode--gel (as well as shell-gel) contact 
surfaces.  To reduce computational cost, only the upper hemispherical shell and the complete 
interior volume were included in the simulation domain; the lower shell half is 
assumed to be non-conductive and therefore does not participate in the electric 
field distribution.

A three-dimensional tetrahedral mesh was generated using Gmsh~4.11~\cite{gmsh}. 
Second-order (P2) tetrahedral elements were employed throughout, with 
\texttt{Mesh.ElementOrder = 2} and \texttt{Mesh.SecondOrderLinear = 0}. 
Standard optimization and smoothing were applied (\texttt{Mesh.Optimize = 1}, 
\texttt{Mesh.Smoothing = 10}). Global element size bounds were set to 
$h_{\min} = 0.3$~mm and $h_{\max} = 6.0$~mm.

A distance-based size field was defined to achieve graded refinement near the 
electrodes. All electrode contact surfaces (tip, ring, and sleeve for each of 
the nine electrodes) were collected as refinement targets. The field prescribed 
0.4~mm elements at electrode surfaces, transitioning to 5.0~mm elements at 
distances beyond 25~mm. Fine resolution was maintained within 3~mm of the 
electrodes to capture steep potential gradients near the voltage sources.

Volume entities were tagged to distinguish the interior conductive medium and 
conductive shell, enabling spatially-varying conductivity assignment in the 
solver. Surface entities were tagged for each electrode's three contact regions 
(tip, ring, sleeve) to facilitate boundary condition specification during simulation.
