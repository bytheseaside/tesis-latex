The phantom head model was developed to provide a realistic and modular platform 
for simulating electrical propagation through cranial tissues. 

A realistic mannequin head and torso geometry was sourced from the GrabCAD 
Community Library~\cite{grabcad}. The specific CAD model~\cite{mannequin-model} 
was then imported into \textit{Autodesk Fusion~360} for geometric processing and adaptation.

The phantom assembly was conceived as a modular system composed of interchangeable components, 
each consisting of a base geometry (serving as a template) and an extendable or modifiable version 
tailored for specific experiments. 
The main components are:

\begin{itemize}
    \item a two-part outer \textbf{head shell} representing the skull boundary and defining the internal cavity to be filled with conductive gel,
    \item a set of \textbf{wire guides} and a corresponding \textbf{base connector} model that can be geometrically extended to reach any desired internal position, and 
    \item a \textbf{lid} customized with openings for the active antenna configuration, together with a \textbf{base (uncut) lid} that serves as a starting template for future variants.
\end{itemize}

This modular structure allows each part—such as the lid or the antenna connector—to be reprinted or redefined independently, 
while maintaining compatibility with the rest of the system.

The design emphasizes modularity and reproducibility: individual parts can be reprinted or modified 
without requiring a complete re-fabrication of the phantom.
