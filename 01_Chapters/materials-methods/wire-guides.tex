A network of internal wire guides (Figure \ref{fig:wire_guides_assembly}) was designed to route and position the input electrodes within the head's internal volume. 
Each guide follows a unique, non-overlapping path leading to a selected coordinate inside the cavity. 
The endpoint coordinates were positioned to intentionally break possible spatial symmetries in electrical stimulation, 
thereby enabling more representative and diverse field distributions during testing.

All guides maintain a uniform circular cross-section of 1~cm to accommodate a range of cable or electrode diameters. 


Each wire guide terminates at its base in a specially designed antenna connector that interfaces with the lid. 
The connector geometry features a \textbf{not radially symmetric} profile to prevent rotation and ensure unique,
unambiguous positioning. 
This asymmetric design guarantees that each connector fits into its corresponding opening in the lid 
in only one specific orientation, eliminating assembly errors and ensuring consistent alignment 
of the internal electrodes across experiments.

The connectors are uniquely shaped and numbered at their base for unambiguous identification 
during assembly. 
This numbering system allows consistent correspondence with the experimental layout and the lid opening configuration

The overall design of the wire-guide network enables:
\begin{itemize}
    \item straightforward insertion of flexible antenna wires,
    \item precise alignment of electrode tips within the conductive medium,
    \item foolproof assembly through rotationally-keyed connectors,
    \item and reliable mechanical fixation during gel solidification.
\end{itemize}
