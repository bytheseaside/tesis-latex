A network of nine internal wire guides (Figure~\ref{fig:wire_guides_assembly}) was designed to route and position the input
 electrodes at specific, pre-selected coordinates within the phantom’s internal cavity. Each guide follows a unique, non-overlapping path,
  ensuring that the electrodes reach their intended locations while avoiding collisions or interference between channels.

To support each guide mechanically and to define a reproducible interface with the exterior of the phantom, every guide terminates 
in a dedicated connector base. During the design process, this points were the main concerns: (i) guaranteeing that each guide is inserted
 into the correct opening in the lid, and (ii) ensuring that each guide is inserted in the correct orientation, since rotational misalignment 
 would shift the internal endpoint.

These constraints informed the geometry of the connector base. As shown in Figure~\ref{fig:antenna_connector_bottom}, the base is
 deliberately \emph{not} radially symmetric, providing a unidirectional seating that physically prevents incorrect rotation. 
 In addition, the bases were individually numbered (Figure~\ref{fig:antenna_connector_detail}) to allow unambiguous correspondence 
 between each wire guide and its designated opening.

The inferior lid complements this design by incorporating numbered, unidirectional openings that match the shape of each connector
 base. As a result, each wire guide can be inserted only into its assigned position and only
  in the correct orientation, creating a fully foolproof assembly system and ensuring consistent alignment of the internal electrode tips.

In addition to the connector openings, the lid also incorporates a dedicated filling port for introducing the conductive medium after
assembly (Figure~\ref{fig:antenna_connector_lid}).

Together, the wire-guide network and its mechanical interface achieve:
\begin{itemize}
    \item reliable routing and spatial positioning of internal electrodes,
    \item straightforward insertion of flexible wires,
    \item error-proof assembly through keyed, rotationally constrained connectors,
    \item robust mechanical stability during filling and solidification of the conductive medium,
    \item and direct introduction of the conductive fill material via the dedicated port.
\end{itemize}
