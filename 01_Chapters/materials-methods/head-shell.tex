The outer shell (Figure~\ref{fig:head_shell_halves}) constitutes the main structural boundary of the phantom. It reproduces realistic cranial curvature while maintaining manufacturability for 3D printing. A nominal wall thickness of 3.2~mm was applied in the measuring region, corresponding to the area encompassing the 21 electrode positions defined by the ten–twenty EEG system~\cite{klem-1999}. Outside this region, the geometry was locally thickened, modified, or simplified as needed to meet assembly and manufacturability constraints.

The head was divided into two parts along a \textit{superior–inferior} direction, resulting in an upper and a lower half. The separation interface was carefully positioned to remain outside the measuring region, thereby preventing any discontinuity or mechanical seam from affecting the probe area. To ensure proper alignment and structural integrity, the interface incorporates a male–female mating feature designed to guide and lock the two halves in place. Additionally, an extra sealing groove was included along the joint, allowing it to be filled with either a vulcanized gasket or a bead of liquid silicone to create a waterproof seal. Both the mating structures and the sealing channel are visible in Figure~\ref{fig:head_shell_section}.
