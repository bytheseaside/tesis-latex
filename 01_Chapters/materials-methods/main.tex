The experimental platform developed in this work is based on a bilayer phantom head, designed to support controlled generation and propagation of electric signals within a conductive medium. The system is organized around two primary layers:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The external layer, which defines the overall geometry of the phantom and provides mechanical stability. It is designed using computer-aided design (CAD) tools and serves as the structural support on which electrodes may be placed, while also accommodating geometric features for mechanical alignment and routing of electrical connections.
\item The internal layer, consisting of a conductive electrolyte that fills the enclosed volume. This medium enables the propagation of electrical signals from internal excitation points toward the outer conductive shell, forming the primary conductive pathway within the phantom.
\end{enumerate}

On the basis of this architectural separation, the external structure and the conductive electrolyte are addressed separately and characterized in the sections that follow.
