
Data is typically collected using digital EEG amplifiers with sampling frequencies ranging from 250 Hz to 5 kHz~\cite{cita}. To prevent aliasing and remove noise, signal filters are applied prior to sampling, such as a 0.1 Hz high-pass and 100 Hz low-pass filters~\cite{LEAHY1998159}.

\paragraph{The 10{-}20 Electrode System}

The 10{-}20 electrode system is an international standard for placing scalp electrodes during electroencephalography (EEG) and event-related potential (ERP) studies~\cite{klem-1999}. It serves as a foundational grid for ensuring standardized and reproducible measurements across different subjects and laboratories.

A standard 10{-}20 array typically consists of 21 electrodes. Every position is identified by a letter and a number. The letters correspond to the brain regions they cover: 

\begin{itemize}
    \item F (frontal),
    \item T (temporal),
    \item C (central),
    \item P (parietal),
    \item O (occipital). 
\end{itemize}

The numbers indicate the hemisphere: 

\begin{itemize}
    \item Odd numbers for the left hemisphere,
    \item Even numbers for the right hemisphere,
    \item Z for midline positions.
\end{itemize}
