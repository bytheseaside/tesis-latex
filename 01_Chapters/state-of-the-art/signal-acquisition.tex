Data is typically collected using digital EEG amplifiers with sampling frequencies ranging from 250 Hz to 5 kHz~\cite{veniero-2009}.
Based on the Nyquist theorem, the sampling frequency must be at least twice the highest frequency component present in the signal to
avoid aliasing~\cite{leis-2011}. Given that EEG signals of interest generally fall below 100 Hz, a sampling rate of 250 Hz is often sufficient for most applications~\cite{Naydenov2022}. However, higher sampling rates (e.g., 1 kHz or more) are sometimes employed to capture finer temporal details and to facilitate advanced signal processing techniques~\cite{LEAHY1998159}.
